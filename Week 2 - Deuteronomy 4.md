# Deuteronomy - Week 2

## The LORD Alone Is God

*Before the group meets, read Deuteronomy 1–4.*

# Main Theme

God calls His people to remember who He is, reject idolatry, and respond to His unique greatness with faithful obedience.

# Introduction

After recounting Israel's history in chapters 1–3, Moses pauses before reviewing the covenant and the law. Rather than immediately telling Israel what to do, he reminds them who God is.

Deuteronomy 4 serves as a bridge between Israel's past and the covenant renewal that follows. Moses wants the people to understand that obedience is not merely about following rules. It is the proper response to the God who has revealed Himself, rescued them, and entered into relationship with them.

Throughout this chapter, Moses emphasizes two foundational truths:

* The LORD has uniquely revealed Himself to His people.  
* The LORD alone is worthy of worship and obedience.

These truths become the foundation for everything that follows in Deuteronomy.

# Understanding the Text & the Narrative

**Read Deuteronomy 4:1–14**

1. How does Moses frame obedience in verses 1 and 4, as burden, duty, privilege, or wisdom? What in the text shapes your answer?  
2. Moses says the surrounding nations will see Israel's laws and conclude that Israel's God is near and wise (vv. 5–8). What does this reveal about God's purpose for Israel among the nations?  
3. What specifically does Moses tell the people to pass on to their children and grandchildren (vv. 9–10), and why does he frame forgetting as such a serious danger?

**Read Deuteronomy 4:15–24**

4. Why is the detail that Israel heard God's voice but saw no form so important to Moses' warning against idolatry (vv. 15–19)?  
5. Israel was forbidden from representing God through any image, whether of humans, animals, or celestial bodies (vv. 16–19). What does this prohibition imply about the kind of being God is? Why would any image inevitably misrepresent Him?  
6. What does describing God as "a consuming fire" and "a jealous God" (v. 24) communicate about God's relationship to Israel, and how do they explain the weight of the warnings Moses has just given?

**Read Deuteronomy 4:25–31**

7. Moses prophesies that Israel will fall into idolatry and be scattered among the nations, yet promises that when they seek God in exile, they will find Him (vv. 29–31). What does this passage reveal about the nature of God's covenant commitment to Israel?

**Read Deuteronomy 4:32–40**

8. What is Moses arguing by inviting Israel to survey all of history and ask whether anything like what God did for them has ever occurred (vv. 32–34)? What does he want Israel to conclude?  
9. According to verses 35 and 39, what conclusions does Moses want Israel to reach about God? How do those conclusions reframe the warnings and commands Moses has given throughout the chapter?

# Bringing the Passages Together

10. Looking across the whole chapter, what case does Moses make for why Israel should obey the LORD and worship Him alone? What are the main pillars of his argument?

# Application

11. What do Moses' prescriptions for guarding against forgetting, teaching their children, recalling what they witnessed at Horeb, and keeping God's statutes, reveal about what forgetting actually looks like in practice? How do they translate to the life of a believer today?  
12. As we close, what is one truth about God from Deuteronomy 4 that you most want to remember this week?

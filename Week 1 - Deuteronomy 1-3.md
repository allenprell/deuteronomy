# Deuteronomy - Week 1 

## Remember God’s Faithfulness (Deut 1-3)

# Main Theme

God's people are called to remember both God's faithfulness and their own tendency toward unbelief. Remembering rightly strengthens faith and encourages obedience.

# Introduction

Deuteronomy records Moses' final sermons to Israel as they stand on the edge of the Promised Land. The previous generation had died in the wilderness because of unbelief. A new generation now prepares to enter the land. Before they do, Moses calls them to remember.

In this first study, we see two competing realities:

* God's unwavering faithfulness.  
* Israel's persistent tendency to doubt and fear.

These themes will appear throughout Deuteronomy and provide a foundation for understanding the rest of the book.

# Understanding the Text & the Narrative

**Read Deuteronomy 1:19–33**

1. Why does Moses begin his sermon by revisiting the failure at Kadesh-barnea nearly forty years ago instead of focusing on Israel's victories or successes?  
2. In verses 26–28, the people's fears are centered on the strength of the enemy and the difficulty of the task. What truths about God in verses 29–33 should have changed the way they evaluated the situation? Did the Israelites know these truths?  
3. Verses 30–33 emphasize God's past actions, present presence, and future promises. What does this teach us about the relationship between remembering God's faithfulness and trusting Him in the present?

**Read Deuteronomy 2:7**

4. Why would Moses' reminder that "the LORD your God has been with you" and that they "have lacked nothing" be particularly important for a people preparing to enter a season of warfare and conquest?

**Read Deuteronomy 3:21–29**

5. Moses points Joshua back to what God has already done before speaking about what God will do next. What principle of faith and leadership is Moses modeling for Israel and for Joshua?  
6. Why was Moses forbidden from entering the promised land? Read Numbers 20:2-13. What were Moses’ sins?

# Bringing the Passages Together

7. Why should “remembering” be such a central theme in the life of God's people?  
   
# Application

8. Looking back over the last year or two, what are some ways you have seen God's faithfulness that should strengthen your trust in Him today?  
9. Is there an area of obedience, responsibility, or trust where you are currently tempted to focus more on the obstacles than on God's character? How does this passage challenge your thinking?
